Optimizing Images for the Web

Image is an important aspect in web design/development. It could be a really boring website without images. Large images could slow down websites to load. This could lead users to leave your website and google bots crawls when your website is not completely loaded, which is bad for your SEO.


Even though there are many reasons why websites load very slow, we could start working on our images by compressing and resizing them.


For an example is the homepage of this website. The background image was 149kb, 3000px X 2000px. After it is compressed and resized, it is 42kb, 2000px X 1333px. The self image was 1.3mb, 2316 × 3088 , after it is compressed and resized: 13kb, 400px x 533px.
I have been using a free online image compressor: Optimizzila and to resize image: Responsive Breakpoints. I used them for the background image, and for self image I tried Squoosh and I like it better.
